Introduction

By virtue of the Pro-Bono Project module, I am grateful to have the opportunity to contribute to a project concerning tackling Online Gender Based Violence (OGBV) in Malaysia. My group and I were tasked to collaborate with the School of Computer Science, whereby we provided them legal advice to help them develop their censoring extension. This project has profoundly impacted our perspective of OGBV, opening our eyes to the many real stories of OGBV happening in Malaysia and I appreciate the opportunity to delve deeper in the available legal recourses to aid victims who face OGBV.

The Beginning - Research

The specific goals of our project were two-fold: to conduct in-depth research regarding the initiatives in handling OGBV and to advise the School of Computer Science regarding the topic of OGBV in Malaysia. Our objective was to ensure that their web extension was not only technically sound but legally compliant in addressing OGBV. We were provided a report template by our lecturer, in which six questions were posed for us to conduct research on and in light of this, my group leader delegated the tasks. As a group, we communicated to each other through WhatsApp to discuss further regarding our project. We reminded each other of our deadlines and helped each other whenever clarification was needed. Additionally, we had a shared Google drive where each of our research was available for us to look through and to ensure there were no overlaps.

I worked together with another one of my teammates, where we had to research on the available initiatives in Malaysia to mitigate OGBV. Our research process involved analyzing statutes, searching for reported cases and reading through news articles concerning OGBV. It was interesting to discover that there are many initiatives in Malaysia in helping victims of OGBV, such as the newly introduced anti-stalking law and the Anti-Sexual Harassment Act 2022. Further, I also discovered that NGOs have played a crucial role in supporting victims. Although there are still minor setbacks in protecting victims, I was relieved to know that Malaysia has made so much progress in ensuring women can have a safer space on the Internet.

Collaborating with School of Computer Science

The collaboration with the Computer Science students was a central aspect of our project, in which we provided legal guidance regarding OGBV in order for them to develop an effective web extension. Before we had our first meeting, I expected that they would develop an app to tackle OGBV and I was skeptical on its effectiveness. However, they presented us their idea on developing a web extension which censors any sexist or hateful words. It also acts as a reporting system where users can report other problematic users, and warnings regarding this problematic user would be issued to future users. I found the idea fascinating as I believe it would be an effective way in providing a safer space for women on the Internet. Further, it would notify a woman to be more careful on who they interacted with.

Our group and the School of Computer Science had our first meeting physically so that we could introduce ourselves. Although a little awkward at first, the discussion flowed naturally and our group was relieved that they were very prepared with their extension planning. The next meetings were then conducted online as it was hard to find a time where every member would be on campus. However, I am grateful that we had no issues with communication and the Computer Science students were always consistent in addressing our questions. In Week 12, they presented us with their web extension and I was impressed at how much they have gotten done in only a few weeks. Though currently there are still some limitations as it is still a prototype, I definitely believe it has tremendous potential in helping women on the Internet.

Importance of Teamwork

Group assignments are not foreign to me as there have always been at least 2 group assignments every semester. However, this group assignment posed some challenges as I had to work with law students from other semesters. I felt anxious that I had to work with students that I wasn't familiar with but thankfully, we constantly made sure we were on track as discussed and we made sure that all of our parts were consistent with each other’s.

Naturally, there were some communication issues such as group members not updating on their work, we managed to work through this issue. We made sure that the group members were constantly updated by advising them to always check the group chat and reminding them of any deadlines. Conflicts are normal when it comes to group assignments, but we learned the importance of listening to one another compromising. As a team, we made sure that everyone’s voices could be heard. All in all, it was a good experience to work with students from other semesters and having the opportunity to bounce ideas off of one another.

Conclusion

This project highlighted the importance of teamwork in addressing complex issues. Legal remedies alone are often insufficient, it has to be complimented by technological advancements and practical measures. This was a valuable learning experience, in which it made me realize how my legal education can contribute a difference to the community. It has also made me more educated and aware on the seriousness of OGBV in Malaysia and I appreciate the opportunity to contribute to the School of Computer’s Science web extension, which I have no doubt would be effective in addressing OGBV.
